Kinases and tubulin modifying enzymes

**Kinases that Phosphorylate Tau:**

1. **GSK3B (Glycogen Synthase Kinase-3β)**
    - GSK3B phosphorylates tau at serine and threonine residues, causing detachment from microtubules and their disassembly
    - Tau mutants resistant to phosphorylation by GSK3B retained substantial toxicity and had increased affinity for microtubules
    - Loss of Tuba4a C-terminal polyglutamylation prevents the binding of both tau and GSK3 kinase to neuronal microtubules
2. **CDK5 (Cyclin-Dependent Kinase 5)**
    - CDK5 and GSK3B are the two main protein kinases that have an important role in the abnormal hyperphosphorylation of MAPT which leads to Alzheimer's disease
    - Cyclin dependent kinase 5 (cdk5) phosphorylates tau along with other kinases including GSK3β, CK1, PKA, ERKs 1/2, PKC, CaMKII, tau-tubulin kinases 1 and 2, and MARKs
3. **MARK Kinases (MARK1-4)**
    - Phosphorylation at serine residues in K-X-G-S motifs by MAP/microtubule affinity-regulating kinase (MARK1, MARK2, MARK3 or MARK4) causes detachment from microtubules and their disassembly
    - MARK2-mediated tau phosphorylation led to the dissociation of tau from HDAC6 and reduced tau and tubulin acetylation
4. **FYN, SYK, and ABL Kinases**
    - Tau can be phosphorylated by tyrosine kinases such as the SRC family members (LCK, SYK, and FYN) and the ABL family members (ARG and ABL1), with FYN, SYK, and ABL kinases phosphorylating tau at the Y18, Y197, and Y394 residues

**Tubulin-Modifying Enzymes:**

1. **TTLL Family (Tubulin Tyrosine Ligase-Like enzymes)**
    - **TTLL1**: TTLL1 is one of the two predominant neuronal polyglutamylases responsible for polyglutamylation, primarily modifying α-tubulin
    - **TTLL7**: TTLL7 is the other predominant neuronal polyglutamylase, primarily modifying β-tubulin
    - TTLL3, TTLL8, and TTLL10 represent polyglycylases whereas the specific function of TTLL12 is presently unknown
2. **CCP Family (Cytosolic Carboxypeptidases - Deglutamylases)**
    - **CCP1 and CCP6**: CCPs are tubulin-modifying enzymes that remove acidic amino acid residues, reversing polyglutamylation catalyzed by TTLL family enzymes
    - CCP5 was identified as a functional mammalian tubulin deglutamylase enzyme
3. **HDAC6 (Histone Deacetylase 6)**
    - HDAC6 is a tau deacetylase that protects against tau accumulation, with acetylation of tau inhibiting its function and promoting aggregation
    - HDAC6 is one of the enzymes involved in tubulin deacetylation
4. **ATAT1/MEC17 (α-Tubulin Acetyltransferase)**
    - αTAT1 and Mec17 are the major enzymes responsible for acetylation of α-tubulin at lysine-40
5. **SIRT2 (Sirtuin 2)**
    - SIRT2 is involved in tubulin deacetylation along with HDAC6
6. **TTL (Tubulin Tyrosine Ligase)**
    - Tubulin Tyrosine Ligase (TTL) is the enzyme responsible for tyrosination of tubulin dimers
7. **TUBA4A (Tubulin Alpha-4a)**
    - Tuba4a is a major tubulin isotype highly expressed in the postnatal nervous system and carries the longest polyglutamyl side chains, making it unique within the family of tubulins
