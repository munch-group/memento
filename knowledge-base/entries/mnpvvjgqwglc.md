> Other modes of X-linked inheritance are mainly observed in disorders affecting the central nervous system. Variants in X-linked genes such as *MECP2*[6], *CDKL5*[7][8], and *DDX3X*[9] preferentially affect heterozygous females. These variants usually occur de novo, and hemizygous males are either not viable or survive only if variants are hypomorphic or mosaic. Variants in other genes affect hemizygous males and heterozygous females almost equally. The list of X-linked disorders first described as selectively affecting males but turning out to also affect females is continually increasing and include *IQSEC2*[10], *NEXMIF*[11], *KDM5C*[12], *HUWE1*[13], *USP9X*[14], and *CLCN4*[15]. Lastly, two X-linked disorders, related to *PCDH19* and *EFNB1*, affect heterozygous females and postzygotic somatic mosaic males (due to cellular interference), while hemizygous males are spared[16][17].
