> Bantu speaking populations practicing slash-and-burn agriculture expanded into the forest habitats populated by the ancestors of modern Pygmy populations [[8]](#pgen.1002641-Philipson1). This secondary contact resulted in admixture, predominantly involving maternal Pygmy and paternal Bantu ancestors that remains on-going [[6]](#pgen.1002641-QuintanaMurci1), as well as a cultural exchange that eliminated the Pygmy's ancestral language [[9]](#pgen.1002641-Tishkoff1).
