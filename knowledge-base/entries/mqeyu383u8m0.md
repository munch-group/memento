> We have previously shown that the mouse meiosis expressed gene 1 (MEIG1) is indispensable for spermatogenesis. Males deficient in *Meig1* were infertile, associated with abnormal sperm heads and tail formation. ICSI was conducted using sperm heads from *Meig1* KO mice to bypass the sperm motility issue. Impaired fertilization and failure of embryonic development were observed, associated with sperm DNA damage as evaluated by the COMET Assay. This study suggests a role of MEIG1 in maintaining sperm DNA integrity that is essential for normal fertilization and embryo development.
